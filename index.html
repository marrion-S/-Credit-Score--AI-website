Understanding Your Credit Score

We believe everyone deserves fair access to credit. Here is how it works.

What is a Credit Score?

A credit score is a number that shows how likely you are to repay a loan. At CreditScore AI, we use more than just bank records. We look at your daily life, your community ties, and your character.

💡 Did you know?
Market traders, farmers, and gig workers can build credit too! Your regular payments, savings groups, and community standing all count.

How to Improve Your Score

1
Keep regular transactions
Even small, regular mobile money transfers help show stability.
2
Join a cooperative
Ajo, esusu, or savings groups show you are trusted by your community.
3
Get verified by an agent
A local agent who knows you can add weight to your application.
4
Request community vouching
Community leaders, elders, or religious figures can vouch for your character.

Why Community Vouching Matters

In many communities, trust is built through relationships, not paperwork. If you do not have formal bank records, a vouch from your cooperative leader, market association head, or community elder tells us you are reliable. We respect these traditions and include them in your score.
